Research Interests

My research interests involve understanding and exploring complex social systems using computational methods, including agent-based modeling (ABM), social network analysis (SNA), and geographic information systems (GIS) to model human movement and behavior.

Integration of ABM, SNA, and GIS

The integration of SNA and GIS in an ABM allows us to model agent-to-agent and agent-to-environment interactions spatiotemporally. Moreover, it allows us to explore how social structures (i.e., dynamic social networks) emerge from individual interactions over social (SNA) and physical (GIS) space and examine the feedback loop between individual interaction and collective dynamics.

The Balance Between Theory and Data

A unique advantage of computational modeling and agent-based modeling, specifically, is its ability to concurrently build theoretical and empirical models of social phenomena (Manson et al., 2012). Using a computational approach we can develop “intermediate” models of social phenomena, which are explanatory in their intent to explore theory and descriptive in their use of empirical data to build the environment and create the agents. Intermediate models are flexible enough to apply general theories of human behavior and to test existing theory on environments that represent actual, real world settings. As such, they seek a balance between abstraction and reality. This balance provides a certain level of exploratory flexibility, but at the same time, grounds the model in reality.
