I'm a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Social & Decision Analytics Laboratory at Virginia Tech. My research interests involve understanding and exploring complex social systems using computational methods, including agent-based modeling (ABM), social network analysis (SNA), and geographic information systems (GIS) to model human movement and behavior.

As part of her dissertation, I developed an ABM using MASON (a multi-agent simulation library core in Java) to explore how the dynamics of an urban slum, human behavior, opinion dynamics, and the diffusion of a rumor can influence the emergence of riots. The development of an urban environment using crowdsourced data for the agents to move and interact was a key component of this model, including the creation of the transportation network and the geo-location of facilities. Agent behavior, grounded in theory, helped created dynamic social networks.

Prior to my dissertation research, I spent nearly 10 years working in industry, where I gained extensive experience analyzing large datasets and developing industry ready simulation and statistical models. I hold a B.S. in Industrial & Systems Engineering from the University of Florida, an M.A. in International Business from the University of Florida, and a Ph.D. in Computational Social Science from George Mason University.
